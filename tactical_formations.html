Tactical Formations

Overview

There are countless scenarios in which you would use every single formation I am about to tell you about, but depending on what situation you are in determines the right formation to use to give you and your team the highest chance of survival. In this section I will show you what each formation looks like and when is best to use them.

Wedge Formation

A wedge is primarily used when there is known enemies in the area and provides 360 degree security. This formation can be spread out based on the type of terrain you are on. ie: If you are in a field the distance between each soldier is greater than if you are in a wooded area. In this formation the team leader can also decide whether they want to be strong right or strong left and this is determined by which side of the wedge the machine gunner is located. Youâ€™ll notice that the soldiers are offset from one another and the reason behind this is so the enemy cannot fire in one direction and hit more than one soldier without adjusting fire.

Staggered Column

This formation is used when your team/squad etc. is travelling along a road or some sort of wide trail and again provides 360 degree security. The soldiers are again offset from one another and the reason behind this is so the enemy cannot fire in one direction and hit more than one soldier without adjusting fire.

Echelon Left/Right

This formation is mainly used to protect against the enemy flanking from a certain side and allows for maximum firepower to be delivered in the direction of the enemy flank.

Line Formation

This formation is only used when you are under fire from the enemy. This formation allows maximum firepower to be delivered to the front and gives the team the best chance to gain fire superiority over the enemy and effectively suppress them.

File Formation

This formation is used when time is of the essence because the team/squad can move the fastest in this formation but this also has the least amount of security. This is also used when the team encounters thick brush and heavily wooded areas where soldiers cannot easily spread out. The soldiers do not have to necessarily aim their guns to the left or right but their heads must always be on a swivel in this formation.

Rolling T Formation:

This formation (also known as the serpentine formation) is used when a fire team has to move down a hallway or trench and allows for maximum security while doing so. This formation also provides protection in the rear of the formation. The soldiers can either decide to do a crossfire technique or look straight down the hallway covering thier own sector in order to secure doors or windows that may appear further down the hallway.

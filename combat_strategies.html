Combat Strategies

Overview

It is inevitable that you and your team/squad are going to make contact with the enemy at some point in time so it is crucial that your team is prepared with the proper equipment to execute the mission successfully as well as being armed with the knowledge of what to do in certain situations. This section is going to give you and your team a broad overview of the different strategies and tactics needed to win the battle.

Squad Attack

One of the first things that your squad will need to know is how to conduct a squad attack. This is a simple yet effective tactic to gain dominance over the enemy and eliminate them.

When your squad is taking fire from the enemy, the Alpha team leader (or whichever team leader is leading the formation at the time) needs to quickly get his team in the line formation and rapidly start returning fire in the direction of the enemy. Note: These shots do not need to be accurate because their only purpose is to gain fire superiority over the enemy.

The next step in the process is for the squad leader to decide which direction they want Bravo team (trailing team in the formation) to flank. Once this decision is made Bravo team is going to move out and keep as concealed as possible on their way towards the flanking position. This is all happening while Alpha team is keeping the enemy’s heads down with steady, constant fire.

Once Bravo team is in place, they will signal Alpha team to shift their fire in a certain direction in order to avoid casualties from friendly fire. From here Bravo team will start advancing towards the enemy and clearing the area.

After Bravo team has successfully swept through the area, they will signal Alpha team to pick up and clear the area from their direction.

Once all enemies have been eliminated the squad can reassemble and start evaluating its next moves.

Breaking Contact

Breaking contact is a tactic used when your squad is outnumbered or outgunned by a larger enemy element.

At your squad leader’s discretion, they will call for a breaking contact movement. They will designate which team is going to suppress the enemy while the other team bounds backward to a new firing position and starts returning fire on the enemy. Once the team that bounded is set in place, the team that was originally suppressing fire will then bound back to a new firing position.

This process will repeat as many times as needed until all friendly units have disengaged from the enemy.

Ambush

An ambush is a surprise attack on a moving or halted enemy force and is implemented to maximize the most amount of damage and destruction on the target in the shortest amount of time.

Before you implement an ambush, you’ll want to know where the enemy is going to come from and the size of the enemy force you will be ambushing.

First, you will want to deploy an early warning team further down from where the ambush will take place in both directions of where the enemy may come from.

You will want to deploy your most casualty producing weapons on each side of the ambush so they can trap the enemy in the kill zone.

Then you’ll want to deploy each soldier out in a line each with their own cone of fire.

After this is set up, you will designate a couple soldiers to plant explosives (if necessary) in crucial spots in order to cripple the enemy even further.

Whatever it is that initiates the ambush will be up to the squad leader’s discretion.

Reacting to Indirect Fire

There are a few things you can do to ensure your survival if you ever find yourself under an artillery or indirect fire attack.

One thing you can do is to be alert and listen for the sounds of artillery. An incoming shell will make a very high pitched sound and you will be able to hear the artillery being fired in your direction before it even lands and detonates near your position.

If you do hear the sound of artillery, immediately yell out “INCOMING!!” This lets your squad/team know to seek cover or get as low to the ground as possible.

You will need to move your position as quickly as possible to get out of the impact zone. Your squad/team leader will usually call out a direction for you and your fellow soldiers to run to get to safety as fast as possible.
